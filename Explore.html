Questions about Braille

What is Braille?

Braille is a tactile writing system used by people with visual impairments, consisting of raised dots representing letters and numbers.

Who founded Braille?

Braille was developed by Louis Braille, a French educator who was himself blind, in the early 19th century..

History of Braille ?

Louis Braille developed the Braille system in 1824, simplifying a military code into a tactile writing system for the blind, revolutionizing accessibility for the visually impaired worldwide.

Types of Braille representation

Unified English Braille (UEB) is used for English-language materials, while Grade 1 Braille represents each letter as its corresponding Braille symbol and Grade 2 Braille employs contractions for common letter combinations and words to increase reading speed.

About Braille writing machine

A Braille writing machine, such as the Perkins Brailler, facilitates the embossing of Braille characters onto paper by pressing keys corresponding to the desired dots within a Braille cell, enabling efficient tactile writing for individuals with visual impairments.
